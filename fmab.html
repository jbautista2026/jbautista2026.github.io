Anime Animus

Fullmetal Alchemist: Brotherhood

If I could choose one series to consider as perfection, this would be it. Welcome to the alchemical world of Fullmetal Alchemist: Brotherhood. Considered by many anime fans to be one of the greatest, if not the greatest anime, Fullmetal Alchemist is a story about two brothers on a journey to regain their bodies after losing them in an attempt to revive their dead mother. In this world, everything follows the law of "equivalent exchange."

"Humankind can not gain anything without first giving something in return. To obtain, something of equal value must be lost. That is Alchemy's first law of equivalent exchange." - Alphonse Elric

Fullmetal Alchemist: Brotherhood is a relatively short shounen anime with only 64 episodes to its name, but those 64 episodes are absolutely filled to the brim with some of the best lore, storytelling, world building and character development you'll ever see. The storyline is right in the middle of "simple" and "complex", making it relatively easy to understand while remaining unpredictable with all the plot twists FMA:B has to offer. However, in my opinion, the pinnacle of FMA:B would be its cast. Nearly every single member of the main cast have so much character and complexity to their names. The way they develop is realistic and far from unnatural.

Again, it's really hard not to sound biased, but this anime really is just that good. It may be a bit difficult to get invested at the start, but once the ball starts rolling, oh boy does it start rolling. Both the journey and the end left me completely satisfied, and I 100% recommend this anime to anyone that hasn't watched it yet.

10/10
